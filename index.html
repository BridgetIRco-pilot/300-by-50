Total sprint work stays at 20–30 min including warm-up/cooldown. This is the highest-yield, lowest-time fat-loss tool you have — evidence consistently shows short sprint intervals cut abdominal fat as well as or better than long steady cardio, in a fraction of the time.

Sledgehammer / "Thor Hammer" Work

This is your secret weapon for the 300-look: it builds explosive rotational core power, lat and grip strength, and doubles as brutal cardio (400–700 kcal/hr at high effort) — while being genuinely fun instead of another treadmill session.

  • Overhead slams — 4 × 10-15 reps each side onto a tyre
  • Rotational strikes (alternating side-to-side) — builds the obliques that make the "300" V-taper
  • Figure-8 hammer pass — grip and shoulder stability

Progression Rule

Add reps before you add weight; add weight before you add sets. If a session leaves you unable to hold form on the last set, that's the ceiling for two more weeks. Deload (halve the volume) every 6th week automatically — it's built into the phase transitions above.

Equipment worth buying (optional)

You don't need a gym, but these unlock a lot for not much money: a second, heavier kettlebell (so you have a light + heavy pair for supersets), a set of gymnastic rings for the pull-up bar (adds huge variety), a heart-rate strap/watch to keep sprint intensity honest, and a cheap tyre if you don't already have one for the hammer.
