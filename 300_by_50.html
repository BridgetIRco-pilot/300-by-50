Comprehensive stack, evidence-ranked. Training and sleep will always outweigh supplements — this is optimisation on top of the work, not a substitute for it.

SupplementDoseTimingWhy
Creatine monohydrate5g/dayAny time, consistency matters more than timingThe single best-evidenced supplement for strength/muscle at any age; timing doesn't matter, daily consistency does
Whey or protein isolate1–2 scoops as neededPost-training or to hit daily protein targetConvenient way to close the gap on ~170-190g/day protein target from 2 meals

Magnesium glycinate300–400mgEveningSleep quality and muscle recovery — sleep affects testosterone more than any supplement on this list
Zinc15–30mgEvening, away from calcium/dairyCorrects deficiency-driven low testosterone (won't push above normal)
Ashwagandha (KSM-66)600mg/dayMorning or split AM/PMModest but real evidence for stress reduction and 8–15% testosterone increase in some trials
Collagen peptides + vitamin C10–15g collagen + 50mg vit C30–60 min before trainingSupports tendon/joint resilience — relevant given sledgehammer and kettlebell ballistic loads
ElectrolytesAs labelledOn sprint/hammer daysReplaces sodium/potassium lost in high-intensity sessions, especially useful in SA heat

Ashwagandha and some testosterone-support ingredients can interact with thyroid, blood pressure or immunosuppressant medication, and aren't recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding. If you're on any prescription medication or have a health condition, check with a pharmacist or doctor before starting ashwagandha specifically.
